About Me

I graduated from Oregon State University with a Bachelor's Degree in Computer Science. I'm proud to have graduated magna cum laude with a 3.75 GPA and consistently made the Dean's List. During my studies, I focused on data science, taking classes in machine learning, database administration, and mathematics. For my capstone project, I worked with the nonprofit PAgCASA, setting up and running networking devices to monitor users' network activity and verify the speeds provided by ISPs.

I participated in the MECOP program, completing two six-month internships. At Intel, I was part of a signal integrity team and developed an app to accelerate post-processing data. Despite having little prior knowledge in this area, I quickly familiarized myself with signal integrity and chip architecture. My app significantly reduced the post-processing time from days to just half an hour.

During my second internship at Collins Aerospace, I worked on creating DevSecOps projects and was involved in an Agile team responsible for the ROAAS system in four different planes. I worked as a full-time team member and obtained SAFe 6.0 certification there. Using Jira's REST API, one of my projects was so successful that it was showcased to other sites and is now being implemented company-wide.

Currently working part-time at Bytagig performing DevOps projects for them and our clients. These projects cover various subjects, from installation scripts to data visualization to setting up MSP and cybersecurity policies. Additionally, I sometimes help provide customer support to our customers whenever it's needed.

With the freedom offered to me from my part time position I have decided to pursue a Master's of Science in Computer Science at Portland State University.
